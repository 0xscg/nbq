Compliance

Built for the new era of building safety.

The Fire Safety Act 2021 and Building Safety Act 2022 have changed what clients, principal contractors and duty holders must be able to prove about their buildings. Compartmentation is no longer a box to tick — it's a liability to evidence.

We install tested, certificated systems to the manufacturer's specification and document what we've done, where and with what — so the information duty holders need is there when they need it.

Good fire stopping is good building physics, too: sealed penetrations and closed cavities improve airtightness and energy performance, and systems that last the life of the building avoid the waste and embodied carbon of replacement — fire safety and net zero pulling in the same direction.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions.

What is passive fire protection?

Passive fire protection is built into the fabric of a building to contain fire and smoke — measures like fire barriers, penetration seals, fire rated ductwork, intumescent coatings and fire doors. Together they compartmentalise the building, slowing fire spread and protecting escape routes. NQB delivers the full scope, covering both fire doors and fire compartmentation.

What services does NQB provide?

Five core services: fire barriers (Stonewall, Firefly and Rockwool), fire penetration seals, fire rated ductwork, intumescent paint, and fire door installation and maintenance — all offered as a full supply and installation service.

What types of projects do you work on?

Residential, commercial and public sector projects across the UK. Key clients include Persimmon Homes, Lambeth Council, Guinness Partnership, Harlow Council and Morgan Sindall.

Do you work in occupied residential buildings?

Yes. Recent packages were delivered across occupied blocks of flats — removing and reinstating ceilings for penetration sealing, working from MEWPs with exclusion zones in place, and making good with minimal disruption to residents.

How do your works support the Building Safety Act and the golden thread?

Under the Building Safety Act 2022 and Fire Safety Act 2021, duty holders must be able to evidence the fire safety measures in their buildings. We install tested, certificated systems to the manufacturer's specification and document the works at handover — what was installed, where and with what — supporting the golden thread of building safety information.

Can passive fire protection support net zero goals?

Yes — the two pull in the same direction. Correctly sealed penetrations and closed cavities improve airtightness and energy performance, and durable tested systems that last the life of the building avoid the waste and embodied carbon of repeat replacement.

Which fire protection manufacturers do you work with?

NQB has worked with all the leading fire protection manufacturers and has comprehensive knowledge of their products and systems — including Firefly, Rockwool, Promat, Magply and Fermasteel systems referenced across our services and completed works.
